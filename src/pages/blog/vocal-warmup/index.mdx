summary: "Vocal warmup before singing not only protects your singing voice but also improves it."

|Exercise|Gist|
|:---|:---|
|Breathing|Proper standup, relaxed posture. Breathe in slowly (5 sec) expanding diaphram (only). Breath out slowly with hiss sound.|
|Siren|Make "ng" or "ooh" sound and glide up and down pitch range.|
|Lip Trills|Relax lips and cheeks. Loose pucker while pushing corners of mouth upwards. Inhale through nose and exhale making "brr" sound|
|Tongue Trills|Like Lip Trills but rolling your "R's" instead|
|Humming|Relax facial muscles and body. Hum "hmmm" sound with jaw open and lips closed gliding up and down pitch range.|
|Vowels|Sing through vowels "Ah, Eh, Ee, Oh, Ooh" on same pitch then step, then glide, up and down pitch range. Keep consistent mouth shape and pay attention to throat muscles.|
|Solfege|Do, Re, Mi, Fa, Sol, La, Ti, Do<br />Up and down scale starting from middle C. Focus on pitch. Then variations.|
|Tongue twisters|She sells seashells by the seashore<br />Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers<br />A proper cup of coffee from a proper copper coffee pot|
|Yawn||
|Jaw loosening||
|Jaw massaging||
